Understanding Our Pulmonology Department

Pulmonology deals with the respiratory system, focusing on the lungs and airways. Pulmonologists diagnose and treat diseases such as asthma, COPD, pneumonia, tuberculosis, and lung cancer. They use various diagnostic methods, including spirometry, chest X-rays, and CT scans, to evaluate lung function and detect issues.

Treatments can range from medications and respiratory therapies to advanced procedures like bronchoscopy and lung transplants. Specialized lung hospitals like Sri Dwarakamayee Hospitals offer comprehensive care with advanced facilities and skilled medical teams to provide the best treatment for respiratory conditions, aiming to improve patients’ lung health and quality of life.

Diagnostic Tests and Care:

  • Fiber Optic Bronchoscopy: Using a thin, flexible tube with a camera, fiber optic bronchoscopy allows doctors to examine the airways and collect lung tissue samples. This procedure is crucial for diagnosing lung diseases, infections, and tumors.
  • Transbronchial Biopsy: This procedure involves extracting tissue samples from the lungs via a bronchoscope. It allows doctors to analyze cellular changes and make precise diagnoses, thereby improving treatment outcomes.
  • Endobronchial Ultrasound (EBUS): EBUS combines ultrasound imaging with bronchoscopy and is a key tool for visualizing and sampling structures around the airways. This advanced technique is essential for staging lung cancer, assessing lymph node involvement, and diagnosing mediastinal masses with real-time imaging and guided biopsies.
  • Pulmonary Function Testing: Pulmonary function testing (PFT) includes spirometry, lung volume measurement, and gas diffusion studies to measure lung performance. These tests are crucial for diagnosing and monitoring respiratory conditions such as asthma, COPD, and restrictive lung diseases, guiding treatment plans, and tracking disease progression.
  • Sleep Lab: In a sleep lab, patients are monitored overnight to diagnose disorders like sleep apnea, insomnia, and restless legs syndrome. The lab records breathing patterns, oxygen levels, heart rate, and brain activity, helping doctors identify sleep disruptions and develop targeted treatments to improve sleep quality and overall health.
  • Pleuroscopy: Pleuroscopy involves using a camera to provide a direct view of the pleural space between the lungs and chest wall. This procedure inspects and biopsies the pleura, diagnoses conditions like pleural effusions and pleural mesothelioma, and offers a less invasive alternative to traditional thoracic surgery.
  • CT/USG-guided Lung Biopsy: CT or ultrasound-guided lung biopsy involves using imaging technologies to direct a needle to a specific lung lesion for tissue sampling. This precise approach is crucial for diagnosing lung cancer, infections, and other conditions, ensuring accurate needle placement and enhancing diagnostic yield.
  • USG-guided Pleural Catheter Insertion: Ultrasound-guided pleural catheter insertion accurately places a catheter in the pleural space to drain excess fluid. This procedure alleviates symptoms like breathlessness and chest pain, significantly improving patient comfort and quality of life, especially in managing recurrent pleural effusions.
  • Comprehensive Allergy Evaluation: A Comprehensive Allergy Evaluation is essential for identifying and managing respiratory allergies effectively. Our Pulmonology Allergy Services offer thorough assessments to pinpoint allergens that may be affecting your respiratory health. This detailed evaluation includes tests and consultations to understand your specific triggers and create a personalized management plan.

Pulmonary Rehabilitation

Pulmonary rehabilitation is a vital part of pulmonary care designed to improve the quality of life for individuals with chronic lung conditions. This comprehensive program combines exercise, education, and support to help manage symptoms and enhance overall lung function. By focusing on personalized exercise plans and respiratory training, pulmonary rehabilitation can boost endurance and reduce breathlessness, empowering patients to lead a more active and fulfilling life.
